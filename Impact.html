What We Do and Our Impact

OUR ACTIONS

Three Prongs of Action: Awareness, Advocacy, and Funding

Awareness

  1. We conduct diabetes knowledge-building workshops and counselling sessions for underprivileged type one diabetic children and their families with the help of endocrinologists and doctors.
  2. We equip grassroots-level women health workers in India called ASHA workers with diabetes capacity and knowledge building, who are each responsible for 1000 people in their community.
  3. Our young team of volunteers use our diabetes awareness modules to conduct workshops in schools, NGOs and government education institutions for teenagers on topics including the symptoms, risk factors and science behind diabetes - for early diagnosis and debunking misconceptions to ensure better community support and awareness.

Funding

  1. We raise funds to sponsor life-saving insulin, syringes, needles and other medical resources for underprivileged type one diabetic kids who need insulin to survive.
  2. We do this through crowdfunding on Milaap, fundraising events, CSR grants and funding from clubs such as Rotary, and Innerwheel.
  3. Our collaborations with the government constituencies and international collaborators and partners like Bristlecone, International We Serve Foundation, and 1M1B Foundation.

Advocacy

  1. We endorse various petitions calling for tax-free insulin in India and inclusion in RPwD Disabilities Act.
  2. We also attended the Virtual NCPCR CBSE discussion on provisions for T1Ds during board exams.
  3. We have been a part of many international, national and state-level conferences-- like the G20 Student Summit, Activate Impact Summit, and Innerwheel State Conference to name a few- where we constantly advocate for our goals and the diabetic cause.
  4. We are actively engaging our community via social media channels like Instagram, Linkedin and Twitter through our engaging content and curate posts to spread awareness and advocate on a global scale reaching millions.

In December 2022, Project Surya effectively collaborated with Karnataka’s ex-deputy Chief Minister to bring diabetes awareness to 10,000+ people in a Midnight Walkathon. Through the walkathon, incremental funds were raised, which will be used to further support the children. The ex-deputy CM addressed the crowd to spread diabetes awareness and later recorded a video for social media supporting Project Surya. The event press coverage plus the video further spread the message. More than 8000 people ran in support of people with T1 diabetes.

Global Health Burden of Diabetes

2019:
Approximately 463 million people living with diabetes worldwide.
2050 Projection:
More than 1.3 billion people will have diabetes.
2017:
9 million people with type 1 diabetes, with 500,000 new cases every year.
Insulin Access:
1 in 2 people who need insulin cannot afford it.
2022:
Over 35,860 people died of type 1 diabetes without receiving a diagnosis.
Economic Impact:
18% of a typical Indian middle-income family’s income is spent on T1D.
Lack of Subsidies:
80% of Indian families with T1D do not have access to government subsidies or medical insurance.
Cost of Insulin:
Insulin is the 4th most expensive liquid in the world.
Social and Psychological Challenges:
Lack of family support, societal taboos, and absence of psycho-social counselling aggravate the issue.
