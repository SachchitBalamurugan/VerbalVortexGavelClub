A mere contribution of at least Rs. 1000 (INR) will help a type-one diabetic child afford and access life-giving insulin for an entire month.Every donation makes a tremendous difference!Type-one diabetic children in India do not have the access to quality healthcare and sufficient resources like glucose monitors, insulin cartridges, insulin pens and CGMs.An average type-one diabetic spends over 20,000 Indian National Rupees on their diabetes every single year.From purchasing insulin, syringes, lancets to diagnostic tests and transport; 20,000 rupees is an inconceivable amount to spend for presumably a daily income worker. Let alone affordability of the essential resources, their mental health is ever deteriorating.From not being able to afford a refrigerator to store life-giving insulin, to missing school due to lack of awareness; the underprivileged t-1 diabetics are facing various problems.Hello! I am Divaa Uthkarsha, a 13-year-old student studying at National Public School Bangalore. I am also a 1M1B Future Leader.Through my project 'Project Surya', I aim to help the underprivileged type one diabetic adolescent living in Bengaluru lead a better quality of life with regards to the management of their diabetes.I started Project Surya as I was inspired by my 10-year-old brother- Surya who is also diagnosed with type-one diabetesWe have a three-pronged approach namely awareness, advocacy and funding.We have conducted various sessions in collaboration with the Samatvam Foundation and the Jnana Sanjeevini Trust (DISHA program)http://samatvam.org/#/charityThrough this fundraiser, I aim to provide insulin pens to the underprivileged type one diabetic kids in collaboration with the DISHA group.So we really urge you to support our cause and support the lives of so many underprivileged type-one diabetic kids.Even a small donation goes a long way!!Because with every donation, one life is sweetened!
